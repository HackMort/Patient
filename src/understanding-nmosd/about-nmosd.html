About NMOSD

NMOSD is a rare
autoimmune disease that impacts the central nervous system

NMOSD, neuromyelitis optica spectrum disorder.

NMOSD is typically identified by antibodies associated with a protein called anti-AQP4

While the root cause of NMOSD is unknown, most people with the disease test positive for anti-aquaporin-4 autoantibodies (anti-AQP4+). Others with NMOSD may not have these autoantibodies (anti-AQP4-) or may instead have myelin oligodendrocyte glycoprotein (MOG) antibodies.

Anti-AQP4+, anti-aquaporin-4 antibody-positive; anti-AQP4-, anti-aquaporin-4 antibody-negative.

The symptoms of all types of NMOSD can include:

  • Blurry vision or blindness in one or both eyes
  • Painful spasms
  • Weakness or paralysis in the legs or arms
  • Numbness or loss of sensation throughout the body
  • Sleeping problems
  • Persistent hiccups
  • Persistent nausea/
    uncontrollable vomiting
  • Bladder or bowel dysfunction

Not everyone experiences the same symptoms. NMOSD attacks can come on suddenly and may result in permanent disability. However, there is hope, as symptoms can be managed with long-term therapy.

While NMOSD is a chronic condition, it is generally manageable with the right treatment
